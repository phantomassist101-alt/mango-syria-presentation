Retail Market Analysis

Syria's retail sector is in early-stage recovery. The pre-2011 nascent modern retail scene was largely destroyed or frozen during 14 years of conflict. Current state: predominantly traditional retail (souks, independent shops), with modern retail concentrated in Damascus.

Galleria Al Hijaz will redefine the retail landscape in Damascus — setting international standards with an entertainment-retail mix that is first of its kind in Syria.

Shaalan Street is being transformed by NDS through the introduction of multiple European brands, reshaping the high-street shopping scene into a modern, curated retail destination.

Total modern retail GLA in Damascus: estimated 150,000–200,000 sqm. By comparison, a mid-tier UAE mall alone exceeds this. The market is severely undersupplied for modern retail.

06

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • First-mover advantage — zero formal competitors
  • NDS owns Galleria Al Hijaz — no landlord risk
  • Existing Mango operator (Iraq) — proven relationship
  • 30-year franchise track record in conflict-affected markets
  • Turkey proximity — fast, cheap supply chain
  • Deep Syria roots — Samha family connections
  • NDS: 1,000+ employees, 90+ POS, 25+ years experience
  • Abu Remaneh site is current Talis store — faster conversion

Weaknesses

  • Cash economy — limited digital payment infrastructure
  • Small addressable market at current income levels (~5%)
  • No established modern retail culture post-conflict
  • Profit repatriation challenges (improving)
  • Energy infrastructure — backup power required
  • Limited consumer data and market research available
  • Staff training to international standards needed

Opportunities

  • 20M+ population with zero international fashion retail
  • GDP growth ~10% projected 2026
  • $28B foreign investment committed
  • 1.2M+ returning diaspora expanding upper-middle class
  • "The brands are coming back" narrative
  • Visa deal → digital payments coming
  • Wedding/Eid seasonal peaks (2–3× base revenue)
  • Expand to 4 cities: Damascus, Homs, Tartous + more
  • Mango global momentum: €3.767B revenue, 2,800+ stores

Threats

  • SYP currency volatility
  • Political instability — northeast tensions, ISIS remnants
  • Parallel imports of Mango goods from Turkey
  • Consumer purchasing power constraints
  • Customs/import bureaucracy and informal fees
  • Energy disruptions affecting operations
  • Competitor entry (Zara/H&M may follow)
  • Property rights/lease enforcement uncertainty
